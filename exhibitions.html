Colour & Light - The Legacy of Impressionism

Colour & Light introduces Impressionist and Neo-Impressionist art from the period 1860-1916. The exhibition shows top names in international art, such as Claude Monet, Auguste Renoir, Camille Pissarro and Alfred Sisley, side by side with artists from the heyday of Finnish Colourism.

Marine Perspectives: A Visual Odyssey

Through the creations of talented artists, such as Claude Joseph Vernet, Iván Aivazovski, Winslow Homer and Peder Severin Krøyer, the exhibition unfolds an artistic odyssey that captures the essence and beauty of the sea in its many forms and encourages a deeper appreciation of the connection between art and nature.

Heart's Reflections: Emotions Captured in Every Stroke

It represents how artists have been able to capture their emotions through art, aiming for others to feel them as well. By expressing their feelings on canvases and sculptures, they forge a unique connection that transcends linguistic and cultural barriers of emotions expressed by the artists.

Beauty of the singular

Beauty of the singular shows the beauty of things in an uncommon yet magnificent way, not very typical to observe. Artists unveil their perspective on beauty through ethereal sculptures, emphasizing rarity as an inherent part of its charm, inviting audiences to explore the uncharted realms of aesthetic appreciation.

Dancing with Abstraction

The essence of this exhibition lies in personal interpretation, where each visitor has the freedom to find meaning and emotional connection with the artworks. It becomes an introspective journey, an experience that celebrates the diversity of perspectives and the magic inherent in artistic abstraction.

All is motion: Extraordinary perspective

All is motion expresses the movement of art, artists play with the perception of their works in an optical way, these works have movement or seem to have it. The exhibition shows different artists, such as Nadir Afonso, Alexander Calder, Gyula Kosice and Marcel Duchamp where they play with your perception of their art.
